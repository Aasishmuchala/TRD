Why It Doesn't Work (Root Cause)

Problem 1: Proxy Data Has No Predictive Power
FII/DII flows are reconstructed from 5-day price momentum (FII = momentum × 150). This is circular — you're using past price to predict future price, but the relationship is already priced in. Real FII/DII flows from SEBI/NSE data would be a genuine leading indicator.
Problem 2: Factors Cancel Each Other Out
On an average day, 2-3 factors fire on the BUY side AND 1-2 fire on the SELL side simultaneously. The engine requires >50 points on one side to generate a signal, but typical scores are 25-45 per side. Result: 99.8% of days produce HOLD.
Problem 3: No Edge in Direction Prediction
Even with relaxed thresholds (≥25), win rate is 42.9% — worse than a coin flip. The scoring rules (FII +25, RSI +20, PCR +15, VIX +10, Supertrend +10) don't combine into a reliable predictor of next-day NIFTY direction.

Bottom Line for a Derivatives Trader

Can this model make money today? No. The quant-only engine with proxy data has no statistical edge. You'd lose ₹3-4 lakhs over 2 years trading it mechanically.

Can it be made profitable? Yes, with two changes:

(a) Real data inputs (Dhan API for FII/DII, PCR, OI) — this alone could flip the win rate above 50%. The current 42.9% accuracy is entirely because the engine is trading on echoes of past price, not forward-looking institutional flow data.

(b) Hybrid LLM+Quant backtesting — the 5 LLM agents (55% weight) provide the contextual reasoning that rules can't capture. Run a 1-month live paper trade with real LLM calls to measure the incremental accuracy boost.

The architecture — 6 agents, weighted consensus, VIX-responsive sizing — is legitimate. The problem is garbage-in (proxy data), not the model design.
